A storyteller’s favorite antagonist, the European dragon is often depicted as wicked, greedy, and merciless. Evil sorcerers would take their form, they would be servants of evil, and dragons would, most commonly, kidnap the kingdom’s fair princess. The trademark fire breathing is a major factor in classing this kind of beast . The other prominent feature is its six limbs; four legs and two wings. Usually red or green, they had been mankind’s most dangerous enemy since the Middle Ages. In folktales, dragon's blood often contains unique powers, keeping them alive for longer or giving them poisonous or acidic properties.

The modern western image of a dragon developed in western Europe during the Middle Ages through the combination of the snakelike dragons of classical Graeco-Roman literature, references to Near Eastern European dragons preserved in the Bible, and western European folk traditions. This popular depiction has held strong throughout centuries; a common interaction is its part as a seemingly unstoppable beast in the famous story Beowulf. Industrial profiteers would use dragons in posters to dissuade riots, governments would print posters their armies taking down the serpent as a show in confidence during war, and popular media like The Lord of The Rings and Game of Thrones feature them as proficient fiends.
Aside from this darker image, the dragon was very popular in representing military family crests for its pride and strength. Being compared to a dragon was paramount to having the strength of a god. Christian texts hold stories of various dragon slayers that are greatly revered, like the popular Saint George. Scholars believe that the fire-breathing element of dragons came from medieval depictions of the mouth of hell; for example, art b y Dutch painter Hieronymus Bosch, among others. The entrance to hell was often depicted as a monster's literal mouth, with the flames and smoke characteristic of Hades belching out. If one believes not only in the literal existence of hell, but also the literal existence of dragons as Satanic, the association is quite logical.
